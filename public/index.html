Ray Tracing Demo

Based on Underweysung der Messung mit dem Zirckel und Richtscheyt (A Course in the Art of Measurement with a Compass and Ruler, 1525).

This image is a famous woodcut by the Renaissance artist Albrecht Dürer, titled Draftsman Drawing a Lute (1525). It illustrates a mechanical device designed to help artists create perfect, mathematically accurate perspective drawings of complex three-dimensional objects.

The device works by physically mapping the "lines of sight" between an object and an observer's eye. Here is a breakdown of how the mechanism depicted in the image functions:

The Key Components

1

The Wall Needle
A large needle with a wide eye hammered into the wall. It denotes the near point of sight.

2

The Long Thread
A strong thread which leads through the wall needle and stretches to the iron pointer.

3

The Lead Weight
A piece of lead that weights the strong thread to keep it stretched.

4

The Vertical Frame
A vertical frame placed on a table parallel to the wall.

5

The Door Tablet
A door hinged to the frame which serves as your tablet for painting. It is closed to mark the spot where the threads cross.

6

The Cross Threads
Two threads nailed to the frame, moved crosswise and in straight lines to confine the long thread.

7

The Iron Pointer
A long iron pointer with a needle's eye, attached to the long thread and moved from point to point on the object.

8

The Subject (Lute)
A lute or another object to your liking, placed so that it will not move while you are using it.

Instructions for the Draughtsman

Step 1: Move the Iron Pointer to a point on the Subject and stretch the Long Thread.

Step 2: Move the Cross Threads crosswise to confine the Long Thread against the Vertical Frame.

Step 3: Relax the thread, close the Door Tablet, and mark where the threads cross on the surface.

An Interactive Demo of Dürer's Drawing Apparatus

Instructions for the Draughtsman

I

Guide the Iron Pointer to a point of interest on the Subject's surface.

II

Click to mark where the Long Thread passes through the Vertical Frame.

III

Repeat the above two steps to draw multiple points from the Subject. Or click the play button to animate the above steps.

IV

Observe how a perspective drawing of the Subject emerges on the Door Tablet.
